IN THE GREEN MOUNTAINS OF PUERTO RICO, SURROUNDED BY THE ATLANTIC OCEAN AND THE CARIBBEAN SEA, DAILY LIFE DEPENDS ON AN ELECTRICAL GRID THAT HAS SHOWN ONGOING FRAGILITY SINCE HURRICANE MARÍA IN 2017.‌

THE ‌PROLONGED BLACKOUTS‌ HAVE AFFECTED HOMES, CHURCHES, HOSPITALS, AND SCHOOLS, REMINDING US THAT RELIABLE ENERGY IS NOT A LUXURY BUT A BASIC NECESSITY FOR HEALTH, SAFETY, AND PROGRESS.[1][2]

BEHIND THESE TECHNICAL CHALLENGES LIES A DEEPER STORY: THE WAY POLITICAL AND RELIGIOUS PASSION HAS SHAPED GOVERNANCE IN THE UNITED STATES AND PUERTO RICO—SOMETIMES BRINGING PEOPLE TOGETHER, AND OTHER TIMES DIVIDING THEM.‌

TODAY, THE ISLAND MUST RISE ABOVE THOSE INHERITED DIVISIONS TO EMBRACE PRACTICAL SOLUTIONS ROOTED IN ITS UNIQUE ENVIRONMENT AND IN COMMON SENSE, UNDERSTANDING THAT DEBATE MUST FOCUS ON DATA AND TANGIBLE NEEDS.

A BRIEF HISTORICAL LOOK

THE ENGLISH SETTLERS IN JAMESTOWN (1607) AND PLYMOUTH (1620) INVOKED PROTESTANT IDEALS TO GOVERN THEMSELVES, SIGNING THE MAYFLOWER COMPACT “FOR THE GLORY OF GOD.”[3] SPAIN, FOR ITS PART, FOUNDED ST. AUGUSTINE IN FLORIDA (1565) UNDER A STRICT CATHOLIC AUTHORITY.[4] FORMED IN 1854, ‌THE REPUBLICAN PARTY CONSOLIDATED A COALITION THAT INCLUDED NORTHERN ABOLITIONISTS‌ ‌AND FOLLOWERS OF THE DECLINING WHIG PARTY, WITH INFLUENCES FROM DIVERSE DENOMINATIONS (INCLUDING NORTHERN PROTESTANTS).[5] [6]‌ DEMOCRATS, ON THE OTHER HAND, ATTRACTED CATHOLIC IMMIGRANTS AND SOUTHERN PROTESTANTS WHO OPPOSED CERTAIN IMPOSED MORAL REFORMS.[7][8] FRANKLIN D. ROOSEVELT’S NEW DEAL GAINED SUPPORT FROM CATHOLICS AND LIBERAL PROTESTANTS THROUGH THE MOVEMENT KNOWN AS THE SOCIAL GOSPEL.[9][10] THE ROE V. WADE DECISION (1973) MOBILIZED EVANGELICALS TOWARD THE REPUBLICANS, CULMINATING IN THE FOUNDING OF THE MORAL MAJORITY IN 1979.[11][12] RONALD REAGAN, IN 1980, ACHIEVED AN ALLIANCE BETWEEN EVANGELICALS AND CONSERVATIVE CATHOLICS.[13] IN PUERTO RICO, RELIGIOUS HISTORY REFLECTS SIMILAR PATTERNS. UNDER SPANISH RULE (1493–1898) CATHOLICISM WAS THE OFFICIAL RELIGION, WITH CHURCHES IN EVERY PLAZA AND SAINT FESTIVALS AS PART OF DAILY LIFE.[14][15] AFTER THE SPANISH-AMERICAN WAR (1898), PROTESTANT MISSIONS ARRIVED RAPIDLY; BY 1916 APPROXIMATELY 1,200 EVANGELICAL CONGREGATIONS WERE REGISTERED, AND PENTECOSTALISM GREW IN RURAL AREAS.[16][17] THE JONES–SHAFROTH ACT OF 1917 GRANTED U.S. CITIZENSHIP AND AFFIRMED RELIGIOUS FREEDOM.[18] IN THE 1930S, THE BEST AVAILABLE CENSUS SOURCE INDICATES THAT AROUND 28% OF THE POPULATION IDENTIFIED AS PROTESTANT (DATA CONSOLIDATED IN THE 1936 CENSUS), THOUGH MANY MAINTAINED STRONG CULTURAL TIES TO CATHOLICISM.[19] THE 1952 CONSTITUTION OF PUERTO RICO ENSHRINED RELIGIOUS FREEDOM, CREATING A PLURALISTIC LANDSCAPE WHERE FAITH MOTIVATES CIVIC ACTION WITHOUT DOMINATING GOVERNMENT.[20]

CURRENT ENERGY CHALLENGES: FACTS AND PERCEPTIONS

PUERTO RICO, WITH ITS MOUNTAINOUS TOPOGRAPHY, HIGH HUMIDITY, AND HURRICANE EXPOSURE, REQUIRES RESILIENT INFRASTRUCTURE: UNDERGROUND NETWORKS IN VULNERABLE ZONES, DIVERSIFIED SOURCES, AND COMMUNITY PLANNING ADAPTED TO THE TERRAIN. FOREIGN COMPANIES LIKE NEW FORTRESS ENERGY (NFE), FOUNDED BY WES EDENS, HAVE INVESTED IN LIQUEFIED NATURAL GAS (LNG) TERMINALS IN SAN JUAN SINCE 2020. ACCORDING TO PUBLIC INFORMATION FROM CONTRACTS APPROVED BY THE PUERTO RICO ELECTRIC POWER AUTHORITY (PREPA/AEE) AND THE ENERGY BUREAU (NEPR), NFE SUPPLIES A PORTION OF GENERATION, MUCH OF WHICH HAS OPERATED ON LIQUEFIED NATURAL GAS SINCE 2020/2021, CONTRIBUTING TO GRID STABILIZATION POST-MARÍA. (THE EXACT PROPORTION OF THE GENERATION MIX CONSTANTLY VARIES BASED ON CONTRACT TERMS, REGULATORY MODIFICATIONS, AND OPERATIONAL CONDITIONS.)[21][22] SOME CRITICS ARGUE THAT NFE’S PARTICIPATION COULD BE PERCEIVED AS A TAX OPTIMIZATION MECHANISM, CONSIDERING WES EDENS’ BROAD INVESTMENT PORTFOLIO, WHICH INCLUDES, AMONG OTHERS, CO-OWNERSHIP OF THE MILWAUKEE BUCKS AND DIVERSIFICATION IN ENERGY PROJECTS.[23] IN JUNE 2023, SEVERAL PUERTO RICAN MUNICIPALITIES FILED LAWSUITS AGAINST MAJOR OIL COMPANIES (EXXONMOBIL, CHEVRON, SHELL, BP, AND TOTALENERGIES), ALLEGING DECEPTION REGARDING CLIMATE RISKS AND COLLECTIVELY SEEKING APPROXIMATELY $1.2 BILLION IN DAMAGES, ACCORDING TO PUBLIC CASE NOTICES.[24][25] THE ISLAND’S GOVERNMENT FILED A SIMILAR LAWSUIT IN MARCH 2024, REQUESTING $1 BILLION IN DAMAGES, ACCORDING TO OFFICIAL COMMUNICATIONS.[26][27] THESE ACTIONS HAVE BEEN LED BY LAW FIRMS SUCH AS MILBERG LLP AND SHER EDLING.[28][29] WHILE OBSERVERS SPECULATE THAT THESE LAWSUITS MAY HAVE DISCOURAGED CERTAIN INVESTORS, TO DATE NO DIRECT CAUSAL AND PUBLICLY DOCUMENTED EVIDENCE EXISTS DEMONSTRATING THAT THESE LEGAL ACTIONS FORCED THE ABANDONMENT OF SPECIFIC LOCAL RESILIENCE PROJECTS.[30] RECENT ENERGY POLICIES HAVE OSCILLATED BETWEEN DEPENDENCE ON IMPORTED FOSSIL FUELS AND THE PROMOTION OF RENEWABLE SOURCES. FOR EXAMPLE, THE PUERTO RICO ENERGY TRANSITION ACT OF 2019 INCENTIVIZED THE REPLACEMENT OF DIESEL GENERATORS WITH CLEANER ALTERNATIVES, THOUGH THE QUALITY, AVAILABILITY, AND EFFECTIVENESS OF SOME SUBSTITUTES HAVE BEEN DEBATED.[31][32] IN CONTRAST, SOME CONSERVATIVE POSITIONS HAVE SOUGHT TO PRESERVE ALLIANCES WITH ROBUST ENERGY SECTORS, THOUGH THESE HAVE SOMETIMES BEEN REJECTED BY OTHERS PRIORITIZING IDEOLOGICAL APPROACHES.[33] EVEN INFLUENTIAL FIGURES SUCH AS BILL GATES HAVE ADVOCATED FOR A PRAGMATIC APPROACH TO ISLAND GRIDS, PRIORITIZING A MIX OF RENEWABLES, NUCLEAR, AND TRANSITIONAL FUELS OVER STRICT IDEOLOGICAL POSITIONS.[34]
