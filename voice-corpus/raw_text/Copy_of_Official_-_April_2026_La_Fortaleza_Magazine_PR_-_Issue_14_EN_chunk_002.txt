April 2026 Adrian Keogh

Founder & President

Fulkrum Studio April Theme:

“Resurrection Reality From

Entangled Suffering to Triumphant Growth.”

(Holy Week Reflection: Good Friday → Resurrection)

Adrian, many entrepreneurs experience seasons where building a

company requires perseverance through uncertainty before success

becomes visible. As the founder of Fulkrum Studio, what were some early

challenges you faced while establishing the agency, and how did

faith and purpose help guide you through those difficult seasons?

I have experienced many challenges while establishing Fulkrum. Everything from rejection, lack of confidence, loss of resources and mental clarity. The Lord provided several redirection moments where I was going one way and with a word of correction, I was now going the right way. Redirection moments are essential to any believer’s life no matter if you’re an entrepreneur or not. A friend who is a retired submariner once told me that when you leave San Diego harbor bound for Hawaii, if you do not come to periscope depth and chart the skies, the currents will cause you to drift to Alaska. So, sailing to Hawaii is a zig zag pattern to correct your course and stay on target. The same principle happens to us and if we are listening, the Holy Spirit redirects us to avoid drifting away.

Moment # 1: I lost my corporate marketing job in January of 2008. With tears in their eyes, my president and COO said that they had to let me go due to the new owners wanting to go in a different direction. They encouraged me to start my own agency saying that I was already running an agency within the company and I had the right stuff to be a successful entrepreneur. The new owners liked my work and became my first customer. That day, I was extremely distraught because I believed that my job was my sole source of income. On my drive home the Lord spoke to me and simply said “read Joshua Chapter 1”. “Be strong and courageous” is repeated four times in the first chapter. “Only meditate on my Word day and night…” This command was the start of my business and anchored me during turbulent times.

Moment # 2: In 2019, my company was facing two lawsuits at the same time

and not experiencing growth, but contraction. My business partner was

becoming angry and bitter, and I was becoming

fearful and self- protective. I was crying out to the Lord because I

didn’t understand why this was happening. I said

“Are you not my Father?

Why are you not taking

care of me?” and instantly the Lord God of

Heaven responded and

said, “I did not tell you

that you were responsible for sales, I told you to seek first the

Kingdom of God and all these things will be added

to you.” I knew that this was the truth and I would

love to say that I was

obedient to God’s instruction, but I wasn’t.

It took me many years to understand and apply this

truth.

Moment # 3: While on a business trip with 20 business owners, I experienced my first panic attack. It was so severe, I thought I was having a heart attack. I went back to my room and thank God I had a room and paced back and forth trying to lower my heart rate. I remembered what Jamie Winship said, “ask the Lord what do you want me to know” and “what do you want me to do.” I cried out in my distress, “God what do you want me to know,” and He responded instantly “I love you.” I said, “What, that’s all you can say? I’m suffering and all you can say is “I Love You.’” I felt disappointed but in God’s infinite wisdom, I learned later the power of that moment, “I Love You.” This concept of God’s love changed my identity as a man, husband, father and entrepreneur.
