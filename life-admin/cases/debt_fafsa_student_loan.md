## BANKRUPTCY CONNECTION (CRITICAL)
Federal student loans are generally NOT dischargeable in Chapter 7 bankruptcy
UNLESS Carlos can prove "undue hardship" under the Brunner Test:

  Brunner Test — all three must be proven:
  1. Debtor cannot maintain a minimal standard of living if forced to repay
  2. Additional circumstances exist indicating this state will persist
  3. Debtor has made good faith efforts to repay the loans

As a Section 8 recipient, SNAP recipient, and person with documented economic
fraud and discrimination in Jacksonville FL, Carlos may have a viable undue
hardship argument. This MUST be discussed with Marggie Rodriguez before filing.

Two possible outcomes from the bankruptcy consultation:
  A) Include student loans — argue undue hardship, seek discharge
  B) Exclude student loans — file Chapter 7 for other debts, then pursue
     income-driven repayment separately

## INCOME-DRIVEN REPAYMENT OPTION
As a Section 8 and SNAP recipient, Carlos likely qualifies for:

  SAVE Plan (Saving on a Valuable Education):
  - Payments based on discretionary income
  - At income near poverty line: payment = $0/month
  - After 10–20 years of $0 payments: remaining balance forgiven
  - $0/month payments STOP all collection activity immediately

  This may be the better path if discharge is not achievable:
  → $0/month = no financial impact
  → Stops wage garnishment or tax refund seizure
  → Counts toward eventual forgiveness
